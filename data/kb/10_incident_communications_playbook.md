# Incident Communications Playbook

Document Type: policy_doc  
Version: 1.9  
Owner: Reliability Operations and Executive Communications

## 1. Purpose

This playbook defines communications expectations during incidents affecting platform reliability, security, compliance, or major customer outcomes.
The organization shall maintain predictable, role-aware communication rhythms throughout incident lifecycle phases.
REQ-1001 Incident commanders must ensure all critical updates are published to designated channels.

Section 2: Communication Control 2
2.1. Control Definition.
The incident organization shall apply Communication Control 2 during active incidents.
REQ-1021 Incident responders must follow Communication Control 2 procedure without undocumented deviation.
2.2. Audience and Channel.
The incident commander shall map Communication Control 2 to internal and external stakeholder channels.
REQ-1022 Communication Control 2 updates must include owner, timestamp, and next-update ETA.
2.3. Quality Requirement.
All Communication Control 2 updates shall use concise, factual, and action-oriented language.
REQ-1023 Communications must avoid speculation and must identify known unknowns.

Section 7: Severity-Based Cadence
7.1. SEV-1 Cadence.
The incident team shall issue SEV-1 stakeholder updates at a fixed high-frequency cadence.
REQ-1071 SEV-1 updates must be published every 30 minutes until stabilization.
7.2. SEV-2 Cadence.
The incident team shall issue SEV-2 updates at a moderate cadence.
REQ-1072 SEV-2 updates must be published every 60 minutes.
7.3. SEV-3 Cadence.
The incident team shall issue SEV-3 updates as milestone-based summaries.
REQ-1073 SEV-3 updates must be published at least every 4 hours.

Section 13: Executive and Customer Messaging
13.1. Executive Briefs.
The incident commander shall provide executive briefs during major incidents.
REQ-10131 Executive briefs must include impact scope, mitigation status, and projected decision points.
13.2. Customer Notice.
Customer-facing updates shall be aligned with verified technical facts and legal review guidance.
REQ-10132 Customer notices must avoid unverified root-cause claims.
13.3. Message Approval.
High-impact external messaging shall be approved by communications and legal representatives.
REQ-10133 Approval records must be stored in the incident timeline.

Section 19: Closure Communication
19.1. Closure Criteria.
Incident closure communications shall define why the event is considered closed.
REQ-10191 Closure updates must include residual risk and next monitoring checks.
19.2. Corrective Actions.
Closure communications shall list corrective actions, owners, and due dates.
REQ-10192 Corrective actions must be published within one business day of closure.
19.3. Post-Incident Summary.
A final incident summary shall be produced for internal knowledge sharing.
REQ-10193 Final summary must be published within five business days.

Section 26: Governance and Audit
26.1. Auditability.
All incident communications shall be timestamped and retained for auditability.
REQ-10261 Communication records must be retained for 365 days.
26.2. Training.
Incident role-holders shall complete annual communication protocol training.
REQ-10262 Training completion must be tracked in the governance registry.
26.3. Drill Reviews.
Communication drills shall be performed quarterly.
REQ-10263 Drill findings must be assigned owners and closure dates.
