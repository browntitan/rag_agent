# AI Operations Control Standard

Document Type: requirements_doc  
Version: 2.3  
Owner: Platform Reliability and Responsible AI Office

## 1. Objective

This standard defines mandatory controls for model lifecycle management, deployment governance, observability, rollback, and post-release oversight.
Each control in this standard shall be interpreted as mandatory for production AI systems unless a formally approved exception exists.
REQ-901 The control owner must register every production model in the model inventory before activation.

Section 2: AI Ops Control Domain 2
2.1. Control Intent.
The platform shall enforce AI Ops Control Domain 2 before production release.
REQ-921 Engineering must attach implementation evidence for AI Ops Control Domain 2 in the release checklist.
2.2. Verification Requirement.
The platform shall collect objective metrics for AI Ops Control Domain 2.
REQ-922 Release approvers must review AI Ops Control Domain 2 metrics prior to approval.
2.3. Exception and Escalation.
Any exception to AI Ops Control Domain 2 must be documented and risk-approved.
REQ-923 Exception approval must expire in 30 days unless renewed.

Section 6: Release Gate Policy
6.1. Gate Entry.
A release shall not enter production until all mandatory control gates are green.
REQ-961 Every release must pass safety regression suite, policy compliance checks, and reproducibility checks.
6.2. Human Approval.
Material model changes must receive joint approval from Platform Reliability and Responsible AI.
REQ-962 Approval records must include reviewer names, decision rationale, and timestamp.
6.3. Time-Boxing.
Release approvals shall expire after 14 days if deployment does not occur.
REQ-963 Expired approvals must be revalidated before production deploy.

Section 12: Rollback Obligations
12.1. Rollback Trigger.
Rollback shall be initiated when severe quality, safety, or compliance thresholds are breached.
REQ-9121 On-call engineers must start rollback workflow within 15 minutes of trigger confirmation.
12.2. Rollback Completion.
Rollback shall restore last known-good model state and related policy configuration.
REQ-9122 Rollback must complete within 4 hours for critical systems.
12.3. Rollback Evidence.
Rollback execution shall generate immutable event logs.
REQ-9123 Post-rollback report must include trigger cause, rollback duration, and corrective backlog.

Section 18: Monitoring and Drift
18.1. Drift Monitoring.
The system shall monitor distribution drift, concept drift, and policy-violation drift.
REQ-9181 Monitoring alerts must trigger triage workflow within 30 minutes.
18.2. Alert Classification.
Alerts shall be classified by business impact and compliance severity.
REQ-9182 High-severity alerts must notify incident communications channels automatically.
18.3. Suppression Rules.
Alert suppression shall be tightly controlled and time-boxed.
REQ-9183 Suppressions must include owner, rationale, and expiration timestamp.

Section 24: Auditability and Traceability
24.1. Trace Scope.
Every prediction-serving request shall include trace identifiers for model version and policy version.
REQ-9241 Trace retention must be at least 365 days for production workloads.
24.2. Control Mapping.
Controls shall map to implementation artifacts and test evidence.
REQ-9242 Every production release must include a signed control mapping report.
24.3. External Review.
High-risk model families shall undergo periodic external control review.
REQ-9243 External review findings must be tracked to closure within 90 days.
