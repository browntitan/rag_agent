# Master Services Agreement v2 (Risk-Tightened Revision)

Document Type: contract  
Effective Date: 2026-02-01  
Parties: Northstar Manufacturing Group ("Customer") and Atlas Cognitive Systems ("Provider")

## 1. Agreement Context

This Master Services Agreement (MSA) defines the revised commercial, security, and operational obligations for enterprise deployment.
The agreement replaces v1 and introduces tighter controls for security, data retention, incident handling, and model governance.
All obligations in this agreement must be treated as enforceable minimum controls, and stricter controls shall take precedence.

1.1. Scope Requirement.

Clause 5: Security Hardening Baseline (v2)
5.1. Encryption Standard.
The Provider shall encrypt customer-controlled payload data at rest using AES-256 and key-wrapping controls compliant with NIST SP 800-57.
REQ-511 The Provider must rotate encryption keys every 90 days.
5.2. Access Approval.
The Provider shall require dual approval and immutable justification logging for privileged production access.
REQ-512 The Provider must revoke privileged credentials within one hour of role change.
5.3. Session Controls.
The Provider shall enforce session timeout at 15 minutes for privileged interfaces.
REQ-513 The Provider must notify the Customer within four hours for suspicious privileged access events.

## Schedule A: Operational Evidence Catalog (Expanded)

- Evidence Type A1: Access control review exports and revocation SLA metrics (monthly)
- Evidence Type A2: Incident runbooks, postmortems, and action closure status (per incident)
- Evidence Type A3: Subprocessor inventory, change notices, and assurance attestations (quarterly)
- Evidence Type A4: Backup lifecycle proofs, deletion attestations, and residual risk statements (monthly)
- Evidence Type A5: Model change validation and rollback drill outputs (monthly)

## Schedule B: Definitions

- "Priority 1 Incident" means an event with total production outage, severe security compromise, or customer-wide service loss.
- "Material Model Change" means a release that alters model architecture, safety profile, expected decision behavior, or risk tier.
- "Operational Record" means any log, evidence artifact, system event, decision trace, or assurance report required for audit.
